Origins

Centuries of Wisdom

The Okere Traditional Council stands as one of the most enduring governing bodies in West Africa, founded in the 14th century. Comprising seven major towns, each with its own divisional chief, the Council unites the clans under the sacred Golden Stool of Okere.

Rooted in the philosophy of unity, justice, and ancestral reverence, the Council has preserved customary law, settled disputes, and guided the Okere people through epochs of triumph and transformation.

Today, the Okere Traditional Council bridges ancient wisdom with modern governance, ensuring the voices of the ancestors continue to shape the future.
